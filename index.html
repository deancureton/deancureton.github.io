Hello, I'm Dean

I'm a student who will be attending Stanford University in the fall after having graduated from the Westminster Schools, and I hope to share my love of STEM subjects to younger kids and help prepare them for their upcoming studies during the school year.

Mathematics

One thing I've always disliked about middle and high school math curriculums is their strict adherence to formulaic problem solving methods. During my studies of advanced math at a young age, I was able to apply intuition and clever problem solving tricks that aren't taught in schools to complete work faster and keep math fun. Through tutoring, I hope to introduce younger kids to these methods that save precious time and keep math from being taught as a dry, formulaic subject.

Courses:

  • Summer Brush-Up
  • Intuition Workshop

Computer Programming

My love for computer science grew from an all too common childhood love of video games. Driven by a curiosity of how they were made, I began programming incredibly simple games, and the satisfaction of bringing my own video game ideas to fruition fueled my passion even more. After having studied computer science for about ten years, I would love to be able to introduce curious young children to the world of programming and game development so that they, too, can find their own passion for invaluable computer science skills that are driving the future.

Courses:

  • Summer Brush-Up
  • Video Game Development
  • Web Design
  • Java Crash Course
  • Python Crash Course

Science

All too often in science classes, students try to absorb so much conceptual information without truly grasping the basics that make science possible. Whether it's in physics, chemistry, or biology, truly understanding the fundamentals of scientific calculation and experimentation is key to efficient work, and I hope to improve students' abilities in these topics.

Courses:

  • Summer Brush-Up
  • Physics and Calculus
